The Problem

No statistical evidence for academic decisions

Grade data existed across programs, courses, terms, and delivery modes — but in silos. Curriculum decisions were being made on assumption, not evidence. I was brought in to change that.

The Solution

A full-stack analytics platform, built from scratch

Designed and deployed end-to-end — data architecture, statistical engine, interactive UI, cloud sync, and PDF export. Non-technical academic staff can run inferential hypothesis tests with one click.

Key Finding Delivered
Statistical equivalence confirmed across programs.
ANOVA (p=0.1684) and Chi-Square (p=0.4762) testing confirmed no significant difference in grade distributions between Master of Management and Organizational Management programs. This validated grading consistency and enabled the institution to expand program offerings without curriculum inequity risk — a governance decision made from data, not assumption.

What Regulated BI Delivery Teaches You
Data governance is not a checklist. It is the product.
When BI outputs are consumed by compliance teams at Fortune 500 health insurers, data quality is not optional — it is the entire value proposition. Two years in this environment produced a BI professional who validates before releasing, documents before deploying, and treats governance as a feature rather than an obstacle. That standard transfers directly to government data platforms.
