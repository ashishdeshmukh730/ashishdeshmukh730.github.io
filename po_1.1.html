The Full Story

A major spirits brand was preparing to enter the US market with a new vodka. They had narrowed their product to three distinct options — different bottle shapes, sizes, and visual identities — but had not committed to one. Their strategy: distribute 50ml sample bottles across randomly selected partner liquor stores, bundle each sample with a gift product, use QR codes on the bottle to collect consumer reviews, and incentivise reviews with a 25% gift card for the customer's next purchase.

When the brief landed on my backlog, I heard the development team's familiar frustration. Every brand activation required the same rebuild — custom store selection, custom variant tracking, custom review flow. Engineering hours consumed by repetition. The brand had a campaign problem. We had a platform problem. And we were about to solve the brand's problem in a way that guaranteed the platform problem would return next month.

I proposed investing sprint capacity upfront to build a standardised brand activation module — configurable, reusable, scalable — and using this campaign as its first implementation. We internally prioritised based on opportunity size, allocated resources, and delivered the framework in two weeks. The campaign ran for thirty days. The brand received comparative performance data across all three bottle designs and made their US market launch decision from real consumer response.
